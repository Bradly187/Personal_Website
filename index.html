// About
BT

I build things, it's always been how I think. Give me a problem and my first instinct is to figure out how to engineer a solution.

What drew me to software was a specific problem I care deeply about: making computing genuinely accessible to people who need adaptive tools to use it, informed by my own experience navigating disability. Most interfaces assume a body that works a certain way. I want to help change that, and I think AI is finally at the point where we can.

The Desktop Agent is the most direct expression of that conviction. It lets someone control a Windows machine through voice, gaze, gesture, tilt, and touch, fusing ten input modalities into something that doesn't require a keyboard or mouse to operate. Accessibility demands the full stack, so that's where I've gone.

My other work, a quantum vs. classical ML comparison for network intrusion detection, a serverless AWS identity pipeline, a full-stack clinic scheduling system, reflects the same pattern: find a real problem, learn what you need to solve it, build it properly.

I'm finishing my B.S. in Computer Science at East Texas A&M University in December 2026, looking for roles at the intersection of AI, adaptive systems, and full-stack engineering, somewhere that sees accessibility as a core feature, not a compliance checkbox.
