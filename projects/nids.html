Network Intrusion Detection:
Classical vs. Quantum ML

Systematic comparison of PCA+SVM, deep learning (MLP), and quantum ML approaches for network intrusion detection on the NSL-KDD dataset. CSCI 459 — AI Enhanced Security, Texas A&M-RELLIS.

// Key findings
MLP (PyTorch) — Best Overall
94.56% binary accuracy, 95.01% F1. Outperformed both classical SVM and quantum transfer learning across all four metrics. Fastest to train at scale.
PCA + SVM (RBF) — Strong Classical Baseline
92.45% binary accuracy, 93.12% F1. Competitive with significantly less compute. Multi-class performance (78.9% acc) was limited by class imbalance in R2L and U2R.
Quantum Transfer Learning — Near-Classical on 4 Qubits
89.34% binary accuracy, 88.76% F1 after ~874 seconds of training. Demonstrates that quantum approaches can reach near-classical performance at current qubit scale — a meaningful result given hardware constraints. Tested on IBM Quantum Heron R2 and Amazon Braket simulators.

Class Imbalance

R2L and U2R classes are severely underrepresented in training data, creating significant class imbalance challenges that suppressed multi-class F1 scores across all models.

Distribution Shift

The test set has a different class distribution than training, simulating real-world distribution shift — a deliberate challenge in the NSL-KDD benchmark.
